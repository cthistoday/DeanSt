The neighbourhood

Soho is unlike anywhere else in London. Compact, dense, endlessly surprising — it has been the city's creative and bohemian heartland for over a century, and it shows no signs of losing its edge.

Deans Street sits at the very centre of it. Walk out of the front door and within a minute you're in the thick of it: the smell of fresh coffee from a dozen independent cafés, the rumble of deliveries to Michelin-starred kitchens, the neon signs of jazz bars that have been open since the 1950s. It's a neighbourhood that genuinely never sleeps — and living here puts you at the heart of one of the world's great cities.

Yet for all its energy, Soho also has a neighbourhood feel that surprises most people. Locals know the butcher on Berwick Street, the barista who saves them a seat, the best quiet table at the back of the French House on a Tuesday afternoon. This is London at its most alive — and Townsend House puts you right in the middle of it.

Food & drink
🍽️
Restaurants
Soho has one of the highest concentrations of good restaurants anywhere in Europe — from corner ramen joints to Michelin-starred rooms, all within walking distance.
  • Bao Soho— Lexington St, iconic Taiwanese
  • Barrafina— Dean Street, exceptional tapas
  • Kiln— Brewer St, Thai wood-fire cooking
  • Quo Vadis— Dean Street, classic British
  • Brasserie Zédel— Sherwood St, grand Parisian brasserie
☕
Coffee & cafés
The independent café scene in Soho punches well above its weight. You're never more than a two-minute walk from an excellent flat white.
  • Flat White— Berwick St, where it all started
  • Monmouth Coffee— Covent Garden, five minutes away
  • Crosstown— Wardour St, brilliant doughnuts
  • Bar Italia— Frith St, open since 1949
  • Milk Beach— Noel St, Australian-style all-day
🍸
Bars & pubs
From legendary literary pubs to intimate cocktail bars, Soho's drinking scene is unmatched. You'll find your own regulars within the first week.
  • The French House— Dean St, Soho institution since 1914
  • Ronnie Scott's Jazz Club— Frith St, world-famous
  • Swift— Old Compton St, award-winning cocktails
  • Milk & Honey— Poland St, speakeasy-style
  • The Dog & Duck— Bateman St, classic Victorian pub
🛍️
Shopping & markets
Carnaby Street, Oxford Street, and Berwick Street Market are on your doorstep. For groceries, Soho has independents for everything.
  • Berwick Street Market— fruit, veg & street food
  • Carnaby Street— fashion & lifestyle, 3 min walk
  • Oxford Street— major retail, 8 min walk
  • Foyles— Charing Cross Rd, legendary bookshop
  • Gosh! Comics— Berwick St, cult graphic novels

Arts & culture
🎭
Theatre & film
The West End is literally on your doorstep. Shaftesbury Avenue — London's theatre district — is a four-minute walk. The Prince Charles Cinema on Leicester Square shows cult classics and arthouse films nightly.
  • Shaftesbury Avenue— 4 min, West End theatres
  • Prince Charles Cinema— 6 min, cult & arthouse
  • Soho Theatre— Dean St, cutting-edge new work
  • Curzon Soho— Shaftesbury Ave, indie cinema
🎵
Music & nightlife
Soho remains one of London's great music neighbourhoods. From jazz to electronic, the venues within ten minutes of Deans Street cover every taste.
  • Ronnie Scott's— Frith St, world-class jazz nightly
  • 100 Club— Oxford St, legendary live music
  • Fabric— Farringdon, 20 min, iconic club
  • Pizza Express Jazz Club— Dean St, intimate live jazz
